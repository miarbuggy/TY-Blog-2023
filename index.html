The TY Program at RedHat

@miaxpinkroses March 20th 2023

The TY Programme at RedHat is an outreach program to let TY students have a taste of what a career in software engineering can be like. The intake is 10 students and the programme lasts 5 days. During the programme, the TY students are introduced to many things, such as mark-up languages, Git and GitHub, Python coding as well as the work ethics and teamwork required to make the office work and the software tick.

Each TY must write a blog based on their experience and thoughts on their potential career in software programming. Below we see the blog of Mia Buggy, one of the TYs in this year's programme.

Day One and Two

@miaxpinkroses on March 21, 2023

On day one, I was very nervous, but I gradually got more comfortable throughout the day. We got all set up, had an introductory meeting and then had a break where we played a couple rounds of pool. I came so close to winning. I also had my cup overflow at the coffee machine, which was so embarassing. Not doing that again. After our lunch/break, we had a Git and GitHub workshop where we were introduced to Git and GitHub, which are forms of version control. Then we had an introduction to HTML and CSS (which I have previous knowlege on, not to flex or anything.) We now have to work on our blogs, which we update throughout the week.

On day two (today), we had a talk abot agility. We had a Microbit Workshop coming up after lunch, I had soup if you must know and I still can't stop laughing about Darragh falling off his bicycle and even drew it on the whiteboard. I was literally laughing the whole way through the pool game. Then at the MicroBit workshop I made a game about Darragh falling off his bicycle. This was written ny me while eating a Nature Valley bar. P.S. What makes it worse about my cup overflowing yesterday is that there is actually a mocha option on the machine.
